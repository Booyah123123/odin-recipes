Fried dumplings

(Image credit to Two Plaid Aprons)

Description

Fried dumplings are a delicious appetizer or snack made by wrapping seasoned meat and vegetables in a thin
dough and pan-frying them until crispy and golden on the outside. Often served with dipping sauce, these
dumplings are common in Chinese takeout. Their crispiness makes them a popular and flavorful dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 pack of dumpling wrappers
  • 1/2 lb. of ground pork
  • 1 cup of chopped cabbage
  • 2 stalks of chopped green onions (scallions)
  • 1 tsp. of grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tbsp. of soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp. of sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp. of sugar
  • 2 tbsp. of vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Prepare the filling:
    • Combine ground pork, chopped cabbage, green onions, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, and
      salt in a bowl, and mix contents evenly.
  2. Make the dumplings:
    • Place a dumpling wrapper on your palm.
    • Put about 1 tsp. of filling in the center.
    • Wet the edge of the wrapper with a little water.
    • Press the edges firmly together, making sure that they are sealed tightly.
    • Repeat until all filling is used.
  3. Fry the dumplings:
    • Heat 1 tbsp. of vegetable oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat.
    • Place the dumplings in the pan, flat side down and spaced out. Make sure they do not touch or
      they will stick to each other.
    • Fry the dumplings for 2-3 minutes or until the bottom sides are golden brown.
    • Add 1/2 cup of water to the pan and cover the pan with a lid.
    • Let dumplings steam for 5-7 minutes.
    • Remove the lid and cook for another minute to leave the bottoms crispy.
  4. Serve on plate:
    • Arrange the dumplings onto a plate and serve with choice of sauce.
    • Enjoy your fried dumplings!
